# Super User Impact — Expert Interpretation Guide

## What This Report Measures

The **Super User Impact** report measures the **actual behavioral and productivity changes** associated with being a Copilot super user. It goes beyond adoption metrics to answer: *Is Copilot actually changing how our super users work, and what is that worth?*

This is the **ROI and business case report** — use it to build executive buy-in, quantify value, justify further investment, and identify which roles and workflows deliver the strongest returns.

**Data source:** Viva Insights behavioral data (meeting hours, email time, document time, collaboration patterns)
**Important caveat:** This is correlation data — super users self-selected into heavy use. The report shows impact *associated with* super user status, not a randomized controlled study. Be transparent about this.

## Key Metrics and Benchmarks

### Hours Saved per Super User per Week
**Estimated based on behavioral change in Viva Insights**
- 🔴 **< 1 hr/week** — Marginal. Either super users aren't yet leveraging high-impact workflows, or measurement window is too short
- 🟡 **1–2 hrs/week** — Moderate. Typical early-stage super user value
- 🟢 **2–4 hrs/week** — Strong. Above Microsoft's median observed range
- 🌟 **> 4 hrs/week** — Exceptional. Workflows are deeply integrated

### Annualized Value per Licensed User (at full super user scale)
**Formula used in report:** Hours saved/week × 52 × average burdened labor rate (org-specific)
A common reference rate is $75–$100/hr for knowledge workers.
- At 2 hrs/week × $75/hr × 52 weeks = **$7,800/year per user** if all licensees become super users
- Frame as: "If we bring [X%] of current licensees to super user level, annualized value = $[Y]M"

### Meeting Hours Delta (super users vs. non-super)
- Super users typically show 15–30 min/week **reduction** in unproductive meeting time (prep + follow-up) through meeting summary and action item automation
- A **> 30 min/week reduction** is a strong signal

### Email Time Delta
- Typical: 20–40 min/week saved through email drafting, summarization
- A **> 40 min/week reduction** is exceptional

### Cross-team Collaboration Change
- Super users often show **increased** cross-team collaboration (because Copilot makes reaching out easier)
- This is a **positive signal** even if it temporarily increases meeting volume

## The ROI Narrative Framework

When building the business case, use this three-part structure:

**1. Proven Value** (what this report shows)
> "Super users in this organization save an average of [X] hours/week — verified through behavioral data, not survey estimates."

**2. Scaling Math** (extrapolation)
> "If we bring [Y% additional users] to super user status, the additional annualized value would be [$Z million], based on this organization's labor costs."

**3. Investment Ask** (what it takes)
> "Achieving this requires [specific program — e.g., 90-day champions extension to Legal and Finance, executive sponsorship, 2 hours/employee of workflow training]. The ROI at [X months] would be [ratio]."

## Interpretation Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Meeting Efficiency Story"
**Signals:** Largest hours-saved category is "meeting prep + follow-up"
**Interpretation:** Meeting Recap, Copilot in Teams, and Follow-up features are the primary value drivers
**Narrative:** "The primary time recovery is in meetings — [X] minutes per meeting × [Y meetings/week] = [Z hrs/week] for super users. For an organization with [N] meeting-heavy roles, this is the highest-ROI use case to scale."

### Pattern 2: "Writing Productivity Story"
**Signals:** Largest hours-saved category is "document/email creation"
**Interpretation:** Copilot in Word, Outlook, and PowerPoint are driving the largest behavioral change
**Narrative:** "Super users are reclaiming [X] hours/week that previously went to drafting — emails, documents, proposals. Roles with high writing volume (Sales, Marketing, HR) are natural expansion targets."

### Pattern 3: "Bright Spot vs. Laggard Gap"
**Signals:** 2–3 teams showing 3x+ the organization's average hours saved
**Interpretation:** Those teams have found workflow-specific use cases that others haven't discovered yet
**Narrative:** "Sales and Customer Success show [X] hrs/week savings vs. the [Y] hr org average. The delta isn't about motivation — it's about use case fit. Extracting and distributing their specific Copilot workflows across the org is the fastest path to value."

### Pattern 4: "Value Without Scale"
**Signals:** High per-user impact, but very few super users
**Interpretation:** The value signal is real but impact is limited by volume — the story is "scale this urgently"
**Narrative:** "Our [N] super users demonstrate [X] hrs/week savings — a compelling signal. But they represent only [Y%] of the licensed base. The business case for accelerating adoption from [Y%] to [40%] is a [$Z]M annualized value opportunity."

## Executive Summary Framing

For Super User Impact, prioritize:
1. Headline hours saved per super user per week (with annualized value)
2. Primary workflow driving the savings
3. Bright spot team / use case
4. Extrapolated org-wide value at scale
5. Gap between current super user % and what's achievable

**Good examples:**
- "Super users save an average of 3.2 hours per week — equivalent to $12,480/user/year at org labor rates"
- "Meeting efficiency is the #1 value driver: 47 min/week saved in prep and follow-up alone"
- "Sales super users lead at 4.1 hrs/week — 28% above average — driven by proposal drafting and deal summary automation"
- "Scaling current super user habits to 40% of licensees would generate $4.2M in annualized time value"

## Recommendations Templates

1. **Scale the bright spot:** "Package the [top 2 teams'] Copilot workflows as role-specific playbooks and deploy them to the [bottom quartile departments with similar job profiles]"
2. **Targeted activation:** "Focus expansion on [roles with highest meeting burden — e.g., managers, PMs, sales] where the meeting efficiency gains shown in this report will have the highest immediate impact"
3. **Business case to leadership:** "Present the [$X M annualized] value opportunity to [C-suite] and request executive sponsorship for a formal 90-day scale-up program targeting [N] additional users"
4. **Continuous measurement:** "Establish a monthly Viva Insights review cadence to track hours saved per cohort — use the trend as the leading KPI for the expansion program"
5. **Sentiment + behavioral alignment:** "Survey the [N super users in this report] on their top 3 use cases and publish internally as proof points — social proof accelerates adoption faster than training"

## Analyst Mode — Extra Slides

1. **Methodology caveat slide**: Self-selection bias explanation, what Viva Insights measures and doesn't measure, privacy thresholds
2. **ROI calculation detail**: Full formula, assumptions, sensitivity table (low/mid/high burdened rate)
3. **Per-workflow breakdown**: Slide for each major Copilot surface (Teams, Outlook, Word, Excel) with specific impact signal
4. **Cohort comparison table**: Hours saved × role type × seniority level (if data available)
5. **Change management implication**: Different training investment levels lead to different hours-saved outcomes — show the curve
6. **Sentiment data (if present)**: How satisfaction scores correlate with hours saved — the human side of the ROI story

## Common Questions This Report Answers

- "Is Copilot actually saving time or just adding noise?" → Hours saved/week per super user
- "What is this worth in dollars?" → Annualized value calculation
- "Which workflows deliver the most value?" → Time saved by Copilot activity category
- "Which teams or roles should we target first?" → Bright spots (highest impact), then role similarity to replicate
- "How do I present ROI to the CFO?" → Hours × labor rate × scale factor
- "Is Copilot changing how people work, or just cosmetically?" → Behavioral pattern shifts (meeting time, collaboration changes)
